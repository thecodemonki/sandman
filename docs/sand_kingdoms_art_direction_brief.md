# SAND KINGDOMS — Starter Camp Art Direction Brief

Purpose: give whoever executes the visual/UI pass an actual direction to aim at, not just "make it nice." This covers mood, palette, materials, lighting, and how it should evolve — not code.

## 1. The core idea: humble, not grand

This is an E-rank starter camp, not a kingdom yet. The player just started. Visually, it should read as scrappy and lived-in — a survivor's foothold in the desert, not a fortress. That's not a limitation, it's the point: the whole manhwa power-fantasy arc (weak → secretly OP) needs a visual "before" to make the "after" land. If the starter camp already looks impressive, base-leveling has nowhere to go.

Reference touchpoints (tone, not literal copying): a Bedouin desert camp — low walls, cloth awnings, rope, worn wood. Think "one person's outpost," not "castle."

## 2. Color palette

- Structures: warm ambers, sun-bleached browns, sandstone tan — matches the existing Desert Dunes biome identity already locked for the world
- Sky/lighting: dusk-leaning — deep blue-purple horizon fading into warm amber near the ground. This is doing double duty: it's atmospheric, and the warm/cool contrast is what makes the amber structures pop instead of looking flat and beige-on-beige
- One accent color, used sparingly: a small cyan/teal glow (crystal-lantern, campfire embers with a slight unnatural tint) — a subtle visual hint at the "hidden power" theme, something that'll matter more once Awakening exists. Don't overuse it; it should read as a small detail, not a color scheme.
- Avoid: bright saturated primary colors, anything that reads "toy-like" default Roblox. Keep saturation moderate, lean warm/neutral.

## 3. Lighting/atmosphere (cheap, high-impact — pure Studio settings)

- Time of day: dusk/golden-hour, not midday. Long shadows, warm rim lighting on structures.
- Light fog in the distance (subtle, not heavy) to add depth and hide the horizon's emptiness
- This is a Lighting-service settings pass, not modeling work — cheap to do, disproportionately improves how "finished" everything looks

## 4. Materials

- Sandstone/rough stone for the low walls (not smooth, not shiny)
- Worn wood for any structural framing (crate, awning posts)
- Fabric/cloth for any awning or tent flap — muted earth tones, not bright
- Rope/leather accents where it makes sense (tying down cloth, crate lashings)
- Avoid Roblox's default "Plastic" material look wherever visible — Sand/Slate/Wood/Fabric material types read far less generic

## 5. Starter camp elements (what should actually be there)

- Low sand-brick wall segments outlining the Protected zone
- A modest fire pit as the camp's centerpiece — small, humble, warm light source, not a bonfire
- One simple cloth awning/lean-to near the fire pit, suggesting shelter
- A couple of crates/barrels scattered near the edge — implies "someone lives and works here" without needing detailed set dressing
- Optional: one small banner/flag — a placeholder for a future guild emblem system, plain cloth for now

## 6. UI must NOT fight the world's palette

The "system window" UI direction locked earlier (translucent blue/gold panels, distinct from biome colors) needs to stay visually separate from the warm world palette — the UI should read as an overlay, not part of the scene. Don't let UI panel colors drift warm/amber just because the world is; keep the cool blue/gold system-window identity distinct on purpose. That contrast is what makes UI feel like "information," not "set dressing."

## 7. Progression should be visible over time (note for later, not this pass)

As base level increases, the camp should visibly grow — taller/more walls, a bigger fire, more structures, eventually something that actually looks like a "kingdom." Don't build this now, but keep it in mind: whatever gets built at E-rank should be the bottom of a visual ladder, not a one-off final look.

## TL;DR
Warm sand tones, dusk lighting, humble scrappy-survivor materials (sandstone/wood/cloth, not plastic), one small cyan accent used sparingly, cool-toned UI kept visually separate from the warm world. It should look like someone just arrived in the desert with nothing — not like they already won.
